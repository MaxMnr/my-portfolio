The Ising Model

What is the Ising Model?

The Ising model is used to simulate the phase transition ferromagnetic-paramagnetic close to the Curie temperature of a material. The ferromagnetism is due to the interaction between the magnetic moments of the electrons.
Let us consider $N$ spins $\sigma_i$ on a $d-$dimensionnal lattice. Each spin can take two values $\sigma_i=\pm 1$. We will consider that the spins interact only with their nearest neighbors through a coupling constant $J$ and that they are affected by a magnetic field $h$. The hamiltonian of the system is then given by: $$\mathcal{H}=-J\sum_{\langle i,j\rangle}\sigma_i\sigma_j - h\sum_{i}^{N}\sigma_i$$ where $\langle i,j\rangle$ means that the sum is over the nearest neighbors of the spin $i$.

Solution for $d>2$

In dimensions $d\in{1, 2}$ the Ising model can be solved but the calculation can be quite tedious. For $d>2$, a possible approximation called the mean field approximation can be used. Let $m=\langle \sigma_i\rangle$ the average magnetization of the system. The mean field approximation consists in writing $\sigma_i=m+(\sigma_i - m)$ and supposing that the fluctuations around the mean value are small ($\sigma - m\lt\!\lt0$). Negleting $(\sigma_i - m)(\sigma_j - m)$ we obtain an approximation for the Hamiltonian of the second order. $$ \begin{align*} \sigma_i\sigma_j &= [m + (\sigma_i - m)][m + (\sigma_j - m)]\\ &= m^2 + m(\sigma_i - m) + m(\sigma_j - m) + (\sigma_i - m)(\sigma_j - m)\\ &\approx m(\sigma_i + \sigma_j) - m^2 \end{align*} $$ The Hamiltonian becomes: $$\mathcal{H}=\dfrac{1}{2}JzNm^2 - (h + zJm)\sum_{i=1}^N\sigma_i$$ where $z$ is the number of nearest neighbors of a spin. The partition function is then given by: $$\mathcal{Z}=\sum_{\sigma_i=\pm 1}e^{-\beta\mathcal{H}}=2^N\cosh^N(\beta(h + zJm))$$ where $\beta=\dfrac{1}{k_BT}$. The free-energy is then given by: $$F=-k_BT\ln(\mathcal{Z})=-k_BT\ln(2^N\cosh^N(\beta(h + zJm)))=-Nk_BT\ln(2\cosh(\beta(h + zJm)))$$ The magnetization can be express as a function of the free-energy: $$m=-\dfrac{1}{N}\dfrac{\partial F}{\partial h}=\tanh(\beta(h + zJm))$$ If we consider that $h=0$ (no external magnetic field) we obtain: $$m=\tanh(\beta zJm)$$ This equation has either one or three solutions depending on the temperature. If $\dfrac{zJm}{k_bT}\le 1$, the only solution is $m=0$. If $\dfrac{zJm}{k_bT}\gt 1$, there are three solutions and we find the critical temperature $T_c$ such that: $$T_c=\dfrac{zJ}{k_b}$$ Here what we see is that the Ising model predicts a phase transition taking place at a critical temperature $T_c$. Below $T_c$ the system is ferromagnetic meaning that the spins are aligned and the material is magnetic. Above $T_c$ the system is paramagnetic meaning that the spins are not aligned, there is no magnetization and the material is not magnetic.
